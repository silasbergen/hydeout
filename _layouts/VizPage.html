Racial disparities in the Winona Area Public School District

This visualization was inspired by the excellent This American Life episode, Is This Working? The entire episode is well worth a listen, but the gist of the episode was that racial inequity in disciplinary action translates into racial inequity in academic outcomes. I was curious to see if this association held up, in the Winona Area Public Schools, using publicly available data from the Minnesota Department of Education Data Center. Turns out, it does.

Quantifying 'most exciting'

Game 5 of the 2017 World Series was truly epic. But so was Game 6 of the 2011 World Series! With this visualization, I wanted to be able to quantify: Which game was more epic?

Thanks to the sabermetric-oriented website Fangraphs play logs, we can obtain Win Expectancy for every single event. Changes in these Win Expectancies are termed Win Probability Added (WPA). Comparing WPA across the two games, we can get a sense for which game was truly the "most exciting." The below visualization also looks at the "top 20 most exciting plays" of each game, and finds that Game 6 (2011) does appear to be the truly "most exciting" game.
